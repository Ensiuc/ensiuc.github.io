I am a researcher driven by a deep fascination with how the environment interacts with the genome and epigenome to shape biological outcomes across generations. My early research explored how ecological factors and captivity influence genome-wide variation and epigenetic modifications in natural populations. During my PhD, I used population genomics and epigenomic profiling to study the molecular signatures of environmental stress and domestication, particularly in conservation-relevant fish species. That work deepened my appreciation for how plastic and dynamic the genome can be in response to external cues, and it taught me the value of integrating field-based questions with high-resolution molecular tools. Building on that foundation, in my postdoctoral work, I focus on how preconception experiences‚Äîsuch as stress or environmental exposures‚Äîaffect DNA methylation in oocytes. I use single-cell methylome sequencing to profile CpG and CpH methylation across individual oocytes and embryos, identifying how maternal environments alter the epigenetic landscape prior to fertilization. My analysis includes clustering cells by epigenetic state, mapping differentially methylated regions and genes, and integrating chromatin state annotations to understand the functional impact of these changes. I am particularly interested in how disrupted or resilient methylation patterns influence early embryonic development and long-term outcomes. I deeply love the research I do. It gives me the opportunity to ask meaningful questions about biology, work with powerful technologies, and contribute to fields that matter.

Research Projects

  • Preconception Stress and Oocyte Methylation in Primate Models ‚Äî In my current work, I investigate how preconception stress alters DNA methylation in rhesus macaque oocytes using single-cell whole-genome bisulfite sequencing (scWGBS). I analyze both CpG and CpH methylation across individual oocytes and early embryos, identifying epigenetic biomarkers of stress and developmental potential.
  • Epigenetic and Genomic Basis of Domestication in Delta Smelt ‚Äî During my PhD, I used WGBS and RAD-seq to study transgenerational epigenetic change in fish reared in conservation hatcheries. Analysis Pipelines

I develop analysis pipelines in epigenomics and genomics, applying them to single-cell methylome data, environmental WGBS, and conservation-focused genome sequencing. Below are descriptions and selected visual examples from recent work.

Epigenomics Analysis

My epigenomics work focuses on both bulk and single-cell DNA methylation analysis. I specialize in CpG and CpH profiling using WGBS and scWGBS data, particularly in oocytes and early embryos. These pipelines are designed for high-resolution, multi-layered integration with chromatin state and transcription factor networks.

üß¨ Whole-Genome Bisulfite Sequencing (WGBS)

  • Trimming and alignment using Bismark + Bowtie2
  • Methylation extraction across CpG and CpH contexts
  • Differential methylation with DMRichR and Comethyl
  • GO enrichment and gene-based interpretation

üî¨ Single-Cell WGBS (scWGBS)

For single-cell epigenomics, I analyze CpG and CpH methylation profiles from thousands of oocytes and embryos using scalable pipelines that include clustering, enrichment, and regulatory module analysis.

  • Demultiplexing and processing via snmC-seq2 & allcools
  • Dimensionality reduction, clustering, and differential methylation (MCDS, Comethyl)
  • Module detection with hdWGCNA, TF motif enrichment (HOMER, MEME)
  • Chromatin state integration (ChromHMM)

üß™ Preconception Stress & Oocyte Epigenomics

This analysis focuses on how maternal stress impacts methylation prior to fertilization, and how these changes persist or reset during early embryogenesis.

  • Cluster-based comparisons of CpG/CpH methylation
  • Marker identification for oocyte quality and developmental potential
  • Integration of methylation, chromatin, and transcription factor networks

Genomics Analysis

My genomics workflows span conservation genetics, population structure, and transcriptomic integration. These analyses help uncover evolutionary dynamics, genetic health, and functional diversity in non-model and endangered species.

  • SNP calling, filtering, and annotation using snpR, PLINK
